For West Virginia landowners.

If you own or control a large tract of land, you may have a site worth evaluating. We look at practical development factors before anyone wastes time chasing a property that will not work.

  • Large contiguous acreage, generally 100–1,000+ acres
  • Reasonable terrain, access, and constructability
  • Proximity to transmission, substations, or scalable utility paths
  • Fiber route potential and carrier access
  • Known mining, title, environmental, or surface-use issues

How we evaluate a site.

Our process starts with desktop screening, then moves to utility coordination and site-level diligence only when the property earns a closer look.

1

Screen

We evaluate parcel size, terrain, access, mining history, and proximity to power and fiber corridors.

2

Validate

We look for practical utility pathways, transmission adjacency, scalable load potential, and obvious fatal flaws.

3

Structure

For viable sites, we work toward land control, access, utility discussions, and a development path that makes sense.

4

Advance

Qualified opportunities can be positioned for infrastructure partners, hyperscale users, capital, and phased deployment.

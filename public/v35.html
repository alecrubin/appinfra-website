What We Do

Why West Virginia

West Virginia is being rediscovered — not for what it once produced, but for what it can power next.

The Mountain State has emerged as one of the most strategically positioned places in the country for data center development, expansion, and investment. Its energy footprint is unmatched: deep baseload generation, abundant low-cost power, and flexible microgrid capacity that delivers the scale and reliability the AI economy demands.

Geography reinforces the case. West Virginia sits in the middle of the country's primary AI compute corridor — between Ashburn, Columbus, and Atlanta — placing campuses within reach of the markets driving demand.

And the state has made it a priority to clear the path. Permitting, regulation, and policy are aligned to let data centers launch quickly and scale without friction.

## Design Context

### Users
The page serves a **mixed audience** with one **primary conversion goal**:

- **Primary (conversion)**: Appalachian landowners — farmers, mineral-rights holders, small-acreage owners, regional brokers — who might submit their property for site selection. They are not technical infra buyers; they need plain-language reassurance that submitting is low-risk and worthwhile.
- **Secondary (credibility)**: Hyperscaler site-selection teams (AWS, Microsoft, Meta, GPU clouds) evaluating whether Appalachia Infra is a serious counterparty. They want substance: megawatts, fiber, water, transmission, ownership structure.
- **Tertiary (validation)**: Capital partners, WV state officials, utilities, and local stakeholders.

Context of use: desktop research session (capital, hyperscalers) AND mobile / lower-broadband Appalachian regions (landowners). Mobile parity is non-negotiable.

### Brand Personality
**Grounded · Ambitious · Regional**

- **Grounded**: The company is *of* Appalachia, not parachuting in. Language and visuals should reflect the land — ridgelines, hardwood forests, hollers, mining heritage — without becoming kitsch or "hillbilly" caricature.
- **Ambitious**: The "Mountains to Megawatts" promise is enormous. Infrastructure language must be precise (MW, fiber routes, substations, water rights) and the visual confidence must match.
- **Regional**: Distinct from generic Bay-Area tech-infra aesthetics. Place is the differentiator. Should feel like *this could only have come from West Virginia*.

Emotional goals: pride in place, confidence in capability, simplicity for landowners, depth for sophisticated buyers.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light. Cream/paper background (`--paper: #fbfaf6`, `--cream: #f7f3ea`).

**Palette**: Deep forest greens (`#0b241e` to `#1f4d3f`) + warm terracotta accent (`#c56749`) + soft terra fill (`#f2d5c6`). This palette is *strong* and place-rooted — protect it. It should not drift toward generic tech green or sustainability green.

**Anti-references**:
- Generic Bay-Area data-center marketing pages (Equinix, Digital Realty default templates)
- "Sustainability tech" startup aesthetic (sans serif on white, lime green CTA, grid of icon cards)
- Any AI-slop dashboard aesthetic — gradient text, dark glow, glassmorphism, hero metric cards

**References that fit the brand**: Edition magazine layouts; field guides; topographic maps; old USGS engineering reports; mid-century industrial photo essays (Appalachia photography by Earl Palmer, Builder Levy); New York Times "The Daily" interactive infra features.

### Design Principles
1. **Plain language first, technical depth on demand** — landowners read first, hyperscalers click in. Lead with "submit your land" clarity; layer megawatt math underneath.
2. **Place over polish** — when choosing between a generic clean-tech move and one that reads as *Appalachian*, pick Appalachian. The place is the moat.
3. **Editorial pacing, not product pacing** — varied vertical rhythm, asymmetric compositions, a cover-to-essay-to-CTA arc. Avoid identical card grids.
4. **Restrained accents, dominant neutral** — paper is the canvas; terracotta is the spark. Use the spark sparingly (true 60-30-10).
5. **Mobile parity for landowners** — the submission CTA must be inescapable on small screens with poor connectivity. Don't hide critical functionality.
